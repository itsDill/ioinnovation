Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Dividend Aristocrat?
A Dividend Aristocrat is an S&P 500 company that has increased its dividend payout for at least 25 consecutive years. These stocks are known for reliable income streams and often outperform the broader market, especially during downturns. They represent the most committed dividend payers in the market.
How many Dividend Aristocrats are there in 2026?
As of 2026, there are 68 Dividend Aristocrats in the S&P 500. The list is reviewed annually by S&P Dow Jones Indices, and companies can be added or removed based on their dividend track record and continued S&P 500 membership.
What is the difference between Dividend Aristocrats and Dividend Kings?
Dividend Aristocrats have 25+ years of consecutive dividend increases and must be S&P 500 members. Dividend Kings have an even more impressive 50+ years of consecutive increases and don't need to be in the S&P 500. There are currently about 50 Dividend Kings, many of which are also Aristocrats.
Are Dividend Aristocrats a good investment?
Dividend Aristocrats have historically outperformed the S&P 500 with lower volatility. They're particularly attractive for income investors, retirees, and those seeking stable, growing passive income. However, past performance doesn't guarantee future results, and you should consider your individual investment goals.
Which Dividend Aristocrat has the highest yield?
Yields change daily with stock prices. Currently, some of the highest-yielding Dividend Aristocrats include 3M (MMM), Walgreens Boots Alliance (WBA), and Realty Income (O). However, very high yields can sometimes indicate market concerns about dividend sustainability.
