Crypto Market Cycles 101: A Risk-First Playbook

Crypto can move fast enough to make every chart look urgent. The better approach is to anchor your decisions to liquidity, dominance, and position sizing so you can stay disciplined when the market gets noisy.

June 3, 2026 7 min read Crypto

The hardest part of crypto is not finding opportunity. It is knowing when the market is rewarding speculation and when it is rewarding patience.

Start with liquidity and dominance

If capital is flooding into the market, it often shows up first in large, liquid assets before rotating to smaller names. When dominance starts to break down, the market may be entering a more speculative phase. That is a signal to tighten rules, not loosen them.

Momentum is useful, but in crypto it can turn into noise fast. Respect the trend, then let risk limits decide your size.

Use position sizing as your edge

You do not need to predict every cycle. You need a position size that lets you stay in the game if volatility spikes, a liquidation plan if the thesis breaks, and a way to avoid overexposure when the crowd gets euphoric.

Know when to wait

The most profitable move is sometimes no trade at all. If liquidity is thinning, narratives are changing every day, or the chart is still trying to find a base, wait for confirmation before adding risk.

Key takeaways

  • Track liquidity and dominance before leaning on any narrative.
  • Size positions so a bad cycle does not knock you out.
  • Waiting is a valid trade when the setup is not clear yet.
