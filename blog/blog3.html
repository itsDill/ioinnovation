Tourism Sector: The Engine of Recovery

Thailand's tourism industry, which accounts for approximately 20% of the country's GDP, is experiencing an unprecedented rebound. International arrivals have reached 89 million year-to-date, surpassing pre-pandemic levels and setting new records. This surge is driving significant gains across tourism-related stocks listed on the SET.

Tourism & Hospitality Sector

The tourism revival is benefiting multiple segments of the Thai economy, from hotels and airlines to retail and entertainment. Key drivers include:

  • Visa-free policies for 93 countries
  • Infrastructure improvements at major airports
  • Digital nomad initiatives attracting long-term visitors
  • Medical tourism expansion capturing high-value travelers

Manufacturing Export Boom

Thailand's manufacturing sector is experiencing a renaissance, driven by several key factors including supply chain diversification away from China, government incentives for high-tech industries, and strong global demand for Thai products. The country's automotive and electronics exports have reached record levels.

Manufacturing & Export Excellence

Thailand's strategic position as the "Detroit of Southeast Asia" continues to strengthen, with several factors driving growth:

  • EV manufacturing hub attracting Tesla, BYD, and other major players
  • Electronics production benefiting from supply chain shifts
  • Food processing exports reaching global markets
  • Petrochemical industry expanding capacity

Key Sectors to Watch in Q4 2025

Based on current trends and government initiatives, several sectors within the SET Index present particularly attractive opportunities for the remainder of 2025:

Energy & Utilities

Thailand's energy transition is accelerating, with significant investments in renewable energy and grid modernization. The government's target of 30% renewable energy by 2030 is driving substantial investment opportunities.

  • PTT (PTT) - Leading energy conglomerate with strong renewable initiatives
  • Electricity Generating (EGCO) - Pure-play renewable energy expansion
  • Banpu (BANPU) - Coal-to-clean energy transformation story

Financial Services

Thai banks are benefiting from economic recovery, rising interest rates, and digital transformation initiatives. Credit growth is accelerating as businesses expand and consumers increase spending.

  • Bangkok Bank (BBL) - Largest bank with strong regional presence
  • Kasikornbank (KBANK) - Digital banking leader
  • Siam Commercial Bank (SCB) - Technology-focused transformation

Technology & Telecommunications

The digital economy boom and 5G rollout are creating significant opportunities in Thailand's tech sector. E-commerce, fintech, and digital services are experiencing rapid growth.

  • Advanced Info Service (ADVANC) - Leading telecom with 5G expansion
  • CP All (CPALL) - Retail technology and digital transformation
  • Delta Electronics Thailand (DELTA) - Industrial automation solutions

Risk Factors and Considerations

While the outlook for Thailand's SET Index is positive, investors should be aware of several risk factors:

  • Global economic slowdown could impact export demand
  • Political uncertainty ahead of elections could create volatility
  • Rising interest rates may pressure highly leveraged companies
  • Baht appreciation could hurt export competitiveness
  • Energy price volatility affects input costs for manufacturers

Investment Outlook

Thailand's SET Index is well-positioned for continued outperformance as the country's economic recovery accelerates. The combination of tourism rebound, manufacturing excellence, and structural reforms creates a compelling investment narrative for the remainder of 2025.

Target SET Index level: 1,650-1,700 by end of 2025 (4-7% upside from current levels)

Key catalysts to watch: Tourism milestone achievements, EV manufacturing announcements, renewable energy project completions, and financial sector earnings growth.

Investors should maintain a balanced approach, focusing on quality companies with strong fundamentals while remaining mindful of global economic headwinds and domestic political developments.
