Singapore STI ETF Investing: Complete Guide 2025

By IO Innovation Fund Research Team January 15, 2025 15 min read
Singapore Investing STI ETFs Index Funds Portfolio Building

Singapore's Straits Times Index (STI) represents the performance of the top 30 companies listed on the Singapore Exchange (SGX). For investors seeking exposure to Singapore's robust economy and strategic position as Southeast Asia's financial hub, STI ETFs offer an excellent entry point. This comprehensive guide explores the best Singapore STI ETFs available in 2025, providing detailed analysis to help you make informed investment decisions.

Key Takeaways

  • Three Main STI ETFs: ES3 (SPDR), G3B (Nikko AM), and N2IU (Lion-Phillip S-REIT)
  • Low Fees: Expense ratios ranging from 0.30% to 0.60% annually
  • Dividend Focus: STI ETFs typically yield 3-5% annually through dividends
  • Banking Heavy: Singapore banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) dominate the index
  • Currency Consideration: All trade in SGD, perfect for Singapore-based investors

Understanding the Singapore STI Index

The Straits Times Index (STI) is Singapore's benchmark stock market index, tracking the 30 largest and most liquid stocks on the Singapore Exchange. Established in 1998 with a base value of 885 points, the STI has become the primary indicator of Singapore's stock market performance.

Step-by-Step Investment Guide

1

Choose Your Brokerage

Singapore-Based Options:

  • Interactive Brokers: USD 0.35 per trade (min. USD 0.35)
  • Tiger Brokers: SGD 1.99 per trade for SGX
  • moomoo: SGD 1.99 per trade
  • POEMS (Philip Securities): 0.28% (min. SGD 25)
  • DBS Vickers: 0.28% (min. SGD 25)
  • OCBC Securities: 0.28% (min. SGD 25)
Pro Tip: For regular STI ETF investing, Interactive Brokers or Tiger Brokers offer the lowest fees for small amounts.
2

Account Setup and Funding

  • Complete brokerage account opening (typically 1-3 business days)
  • Fund your account via bank transfer or cash deposit
  • Ensure you have SGD funding for STI ETF purchases
  • Consider setting up regular savings plan if available
Currency Note: All SGX-listed STI ETFs trade in SGD. Avoid currency conversion costs by funding with SGD.
3

Place Your Order

Search for your chosen ETF:

  • ES3 - SPDR STI ETF
  • G3B - Nikko AM STI ETF
  • N2IU - Lion-Phillip S-REIT ETF

Order Types:

  • Market Order: Executes immediately at current price
  • Limit Order: Executes only at your specified price or better
  • Stop Loss: Consider for risk management
4

Optimal Investment Strategy

Dollar-Cost Averaging (Recommended)

  • Invest fixed amount monthly (e.g., SGD 500-1000)
  • Reduces timing risk and volatility impact
  • Takes advantage of market fluctuations
  • Easier to maintain discipline

Lump Sum Investing

  • Invest large amount all at once
  • Historically outperforms DCA over long term
  • Requires market timing confidence
  • Higher short-term volatility risk
5

Monitor and Rebalance

  • Quarterly Review: Check performance vs. STI index
  • Dividend Tracking: STI ETFs typically pay semi-annually
  • Portfolio Balance: Consider STI ETF as part of broader portfolio (20-40% allocation)
  • Cost Monitoring: Track total fees and expenses annually

Risks and Important Considerations

‚ö†Ô∏è Market Risks

  • Banking Sector Concentration: 42%+ exposure to three Singapore banks
  • Interest Rate Sensitivity: Banking and REIT sectors affected by rate changes
  • Economic Cycles: Singapore's economy tied to global trade and regional growth
  • Currency Risk: For non-SGD investors, SGD fluctuations impact returns

üéØ ETF-Specific Risks

  • Tracking Error: ETF performance may deviate from STI index
  • Liquidity Risk: Lower trading volume during market stress
  • Management Risk: ETF provider operational and financial risks
  • Dividend Tax: Withholding taxes may apply for foreign investors

üåè Geographic and Sector Risks

  • Single Country Exposure: No geographic diversification
  • Sector Imbalance: Heavy weighting in financials and REITs
  • Political Risk: Singapore regulatory and policy changes
  • Trade Dependence: Singapore's export-oriented economy vulnerability

Risk Mitigation Strategies

  • Diversification: Limit STI ETFs to 20-40% of total portfolio
  • Geographic Spread: Combine with US, Europe, and other Asian market ETFs
  • Sector Balance: Add technology and growth ETFs to balance banking/REIT exposure
  • Time Horizon: Maintain minimum 5-year investment horizon
  • Regular Review: Rebalance portfolio quarterly or semi-annually

Tax Implications for Different Investors

üá∏üá¨ Singapore Tax Residents

  • Capital Gains: No capital gains tax in Singapore
  • Dividends: Generally tax-exempt for individual investors
  • Withholding Tax: No withholding tax on Singapore-sourced dividends
  • Reporting: No additional tax reporting required for most investors
Tax Advantage: Singapore residents enjoy one of the most favorable tax environments for ETF investing globally.

üåè Non-Resident Investors

  • Withholding Tax: May apply based on tax treaty between Singapore and home country
  • Home Country Tax: Subject to capital gains and dividend taxes in country of residence
  • Double Tax Treaties: Singapore has extensive treaty network to avoid double taxation
  • Professional Advice: Consult tax advisor in home country for specific implications

üè¢ Corporate and Institutional Investors

  • Corporate Tax: Subject to Singapore corporate tax rates
  • Dividend Income: May qualify for various exemptions and deductions
  • Capital Gains: Generally not taxable unless trading is business activity
  • Compliance: Enhanced reporting requirements may apply

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Over-Concentration in Banking

Problem: Investing heavily in STI ETF plus individual Singapore bank stocks.

Solution: If holding STI ETF, avoid additional exposure to DBS, OCBC, or UOB individually.

‚ùå Ignoring Currency Risk

Problem: Non-SGD investors overlooking SGD fluctuation impact on returns.

Solution: Consider currency hedging or accept FX risk as part of diversification strategy.

‚ùå Chasing Short-term Performance

Problem: Switching between ES3 and G3B based on recent performance.

Solution: Choose based on fees and features, maintain long-term perspective.

‚ùå Insufficient Diversification

Problem: Making STI ETF majority of portfolio (>50%).

Solution: Limit to 20-40% of total portfolio, diversify across geographies and sectors.

‚ùå Timing the Market

Problem: Waiting for "perfect" entry point or trying to time STI cycles.

Solution: Use dollar-cost averaging for regular investing discipline.

‚ùå Neglecting Rebalancing

Problem: Letting STI ETF allocation drift significantly from target.

Solution: Rebalance quarterly or when allocation exceeds ¬±5% from target.

üìä Tactical Asset Allocation

  • Economic Indicators: Increase during Singapore GDP acceleration

  • Regional Rotation: Rotate between ASEAN markets based on relative performance
