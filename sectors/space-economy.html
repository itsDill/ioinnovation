Space Economy

The final frontier is becoming a commercial reality. Rockets, satellites, Starlink, and the $1.8 trillion opportunity beyond Earth.

Market Overview

The space economy is transforming from a government-dominated sector into a thriving commercial industry. SpaceX has revolutionized launch economics, Starlink is creating a new connectivity paradigm, and dozens of companies are racing to capture opportunities in orbit and beyond.

The global space economy is projected to reach $1.8 trillion by 2035, driven by satellite communications, earth observation, space tourism, and eventually asteroid mining and lunar commerce.

Key Subsectors

Launch Services

SpaceX dominates with Falcon 9 and Starship, while Rocket Lab, Blue Origin, and others compete for commercial and government contracts.

Satellite Internet

Starlink leads with 4M+ subscribers, Amazon's Kuiper launching soon, and OneWeb serving enterprise markets globally.

Earth Observation

High-resolution imaging for agriculture, defense, weather, and climate monitoring from Planet Labs, Maxar, and others.

Communications

Traditional GEO satellites from SES, Intelsat, and Viasat serving broadcast, maritime, and aviation connectivity.

Space Tourism

Virgin Galactic, Blue Origin, and Axiom Space bringing civilians to edge of space and ISS, with orbital hotels in development.

Lunar Economy

NASA's Artemis program, lunar landers from Intuitive Machines and Astrobotic, and plans for permanent lunar presence.

Key Companies

SpaceX

Private

Dominant launch provider with Falcon 9, developing Starship, and operating Starlink satellite internet constellation.

Rocket Lab

RKLB

Small satellite launch leader with Electron rocket, developing larger Neutron rocket, and building spacecraft.

Planet Labs

PL

Largest fleet of earth observation satellites, providing daily imagery for agriculture, government, and enterprise.

Intuitive Machines

LUNR

Lunar lander company with NASA contracts, first commercial moon landing in 2024, orbital services in development.

AST SpaceMobile

ASTS

Building space-based cellular network for direct-to-phone connectivity, partnering with AT&T and global carriers.

Redwire

RDW

Space infrastructure company providing components, microgravity research, and on-orbit manufacturing capabilities.

Investment Considerations

Growth Drivers: Decreasing launch costs (SpaceX Starship), exponential satellite deployment, government space budgets, commercial demand for connectivity and imagery, and emerging lunar economy.

Risks: Capital intensity, technology execution risk, regulatory/debris concerns, SpaceX private status limits direct investment, and many public companies are pre-revenue or unprofitable.

Key Metrics to Watch: Launch cadence and cost per kg, Starlink subscriber growth and unit economics, government contract awards, and path to profitability for space SPACs.
